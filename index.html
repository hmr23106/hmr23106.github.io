The Billion Dollar Industry

The fashion industry is the most sustainable industry in the world. It encompasses 28 main countries, is valued at approximately 359 billion dollars and accounts for 2 percent of the world's gross domestic product. There are many sub-industries within fashion such as: menswear, womenswear, athleisure-wear, shoes, and luxury goods.

Nike is the highest grossing fashion company in the world. Nike is a U.S-based atheleisure wear company that specializes in gear for all sports as well as ready-to-wear styles. In 2016 Nike's annual fiscal revenue had reached an all-time high of 32.38 billion dollars. In that same year, Nike employed approximately 71,000 people world-wide.

Although many major companies that lead the fashion industry are based in the United States and Europe, the majority of their manufacturing occurs in Asia due to cheap labor and loose labor laws. These companies can employ people in manufacturing plants for as little as 6 cents per hour. To those people working they do not expect to be paid anymore, so that makes the companies more money because their payouts are very low. The U.S. apparel market is always changing, attempting to adapt to customer trends and new technology that will allow the consumers shopping experience to be more enjoyable and ergonomic."
